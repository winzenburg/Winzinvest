# Kinlet Caregiver Platform Assistant

## Hedgehog Alignment

Kinlet is perhaps the most meaningful expression of the Hedgehog concept: **helping people navigate high-stakes inflection points with agency and clarity**. Caregiving for someone with dementia is one of life's most uncertain, emotionally complex journeys. Kinlet's purpose is to make the opaque legible, to reduce the cognitive and emotional load, and to help caregivers make better decisions with less stress.

This is personal. The experience with family members facing mental health challenges and learning differences informs the empathy and understanding that must permeate every feature.

## Project Overview

Kinlet helps family caregivers manage care for loved ones with Alzheimer's and dementia. The platform focuses on empathy, simplicity, and reducing caregiver burden.

## Voice & Tone

| Principle | Guidance | Why It Matters |
|-----------|----------|----------------|
| Empathy First | Acknowledge the emotional weight before offering solutions | Caregivers feel seen, not lectured |
| Plain Language | Avoid clinical jargon; use words families actually use | Reduces cognitive load during stress |
| Supportive, Not Prescriptive | Offer options, not commands | Respects caregiver autonomy |
| Acknowledge the Journey | Recognize that caregiving is a marathon, not a sprint | Builds trust over time |

- Create PRs for improvements (don't push live—I'll review)
- Draft empathetic copy for new features before development begins

## Decision Framework for Kinlet

When evaluating features or changes, apply extra scrutiny:

1. **Burden Reduction**: Does this make the caregiver's day easier, or add another thing to manage?
2. **Emotional Sensitivity**: Is this appropriate for someone who may be grieving, exhausted, or overwhelmed?
3. **Simplicity**: Can a stressed, sleep-deprived caregiver use this without confusion?
4. **Trust**: Does this feel like it comes from someone who understands, or a corporation?
5. **Accessibility**: Is this usable by caregivers of all ages and technical abilities?
