How it works

Three layers. One outcome.

Every decision the system makes passes through three independent layers before a single order is placed.

  1. 1

    Regime Detection

    Every morning before market open, two independent regime classifiers run. The first reads SPY momentum and VIX volatility to decide whether to trade at all. The second tracks macro indicators to set position sizing. Together, they prevent entering bad markets entirely.

  2. 2

    Risk-Gated Signal Selection

    Screeners identify candidates across six strategies. Each signal then passes 147 sequential risk gates — checking correlation, drawdown, buying-power usage, sector concentration, and more. Only trades that clear every gate get placed.

  3. 3

    Automated Execution & Monitoring

    Qualifying trades go straight to IBKR. The system monitors intraday, managing stop-losses, profit targets, roll timing on options, and position reopens. You receive a daily brief. The audit trail is always open.

FAQ

Common questions

What broker does Mission Control use?
Mission Control trades exclusively through Interactive Brokers (IBKR). You need an existing IBKR account — paper or live — with Portfolio Margin enabled for the full options strategy suite. Reg-T margin works with reduced strategy coverage. The system connects via the official IBKR TWS API; your credentials stay with IBKR.
How does the kill switch work?
The kill switch halts all new order submissions immediately — no new entries, no rolls, no reopens. Existing positions remain open (the system doesn't liquidate automatically). You can re-enable trading when you're ready. The kill switch is accessible from the dashboard header and requires a PIN confirmation to prevent accidental activation.
What does "regime detection" actually mean?
The system runs two independent classifiers daily. Layer 1 (Execution Regime) reads SPY trend and VIX volatility to classify the market as STRONG_UPTREND, CHOPPY, MIXED, STRONG_DOWNTREND, or UNFAVORABLE — and decides which strategy types can run. Layer 2 (Macro Regime Band) scores multiple macro indicators to classify as RISK_ON, NEUTRAL, TIGHTENING, or DEFENSIVE and adjusts position sizing. A STRONG_DOWNTREND or UNFAVORABLE reading stops all new entries entirely.
How do I monitor what's happening?
The Mission Control dashboard shows live positions, P&L, portfolio exposure, regime status, system health, and a full audit trail. A morning brief summarizes overnight activity, today's planned trades, and any risk flags. Optional Telegram notifications alert you to kills, unusual activity, or large fills. You choose how much — or how little — you watch.
What happens during a market crash or black swan event?
Regime detection is designed to catch deteriorating conditions early. In a rapid crash scenario, Layer 1 switches to STRONG_DOWNTREND or UNFAVORABLE, halting new entries. Existing positions are managed by stop-loss orders already placed at IBKR. Portfolio-level drawdown limits trigger automatic risk reduction. No system eliminates crash risk entirely — the goal is to lose significantly less than an unmanaged portfolio.
Can I customize which strategies run?
Yes. Each strategy (momentum, mean-reversion, covered calls, CSPs, etc.) can be enabled or disabled independently. Position sizing parameters, stop-loss levels, and max drawdown limits are configurable. The system ships with production-validated defaults; customization is for users who have reviewed the strategy documentation and understand the trade-offs.
